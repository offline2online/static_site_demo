How It All Connects
Four stages from brand onboarding through to in-store campaign suppression.
01 — BRAND SETUP
Add Brand / Supplier
HQ Admin onboards a brand, uploads logo/icon, defines colour palette, brand guidelines and sets a pricing strategy (Centralised by default).
02 — PRODUCT CATALOGUE
Upload & Manage Products
Products uploaded via JSON or live feed, mapped to brand, assigned categories, pricing set, assets tagged. Enable/disable per product.
03 — STORE OPERATIONS
Retail Staff Manage Stock
Store staff update stock counts, flag Low Stock / Out of Stock conditions, and pin product locations on store maps per floor.
04 — CAMPAIGN OPTIMISATION
Suppress Low-Stock Campaigns
PersonalisationHub reads stock status via API and automatically suppresses campaigns for products that are low or out of stock at a given store.
Platform REST APIs
New APIs exposed by this feature set for use across the Personalisation Hub platform and third-party systems.

Product & Brand APIs

Base URL: https://api.personalisationhub.com/v1

GET
/products
Retrieve product info by product name or SKU (or list of SKUs).
?sku=NK-RN-001 ?name=Air+Max ?skus=NK-RN-001,NK-RN-002
GET
/products/{sku}/assets
Retrieve all product images and assets for a given SKU. Returns URLs, labels and tags.
?tag=primary ?label=hero
GET
/brands/{id}/guidelines
Retrieve brand guidelines, colour palette and identity assets by brand ID, product name or SKU.
?sku=NK-RN-001 ?format=md
GET
/stock/{storeCode}
Retrieve all product stock levels for a given store, including Low Stock and Out of Stock flags.
?store=${StoreCode} ?sku=NK-RN-001 ?status=low_stock
GET
/location/{storeCode}
Retrieve product location data within a store — map reference, coordinates and aisle/zone description.
?store=${StoreCode} ?sku=NK-RN-001
GET
/pricing/{sku}
Retrieve current pricing for a SKU including RRP, active offer price and scheduled price changes.
?store=${StoreCode}
POST
/stock/{storeCode}/update
Push stock count updates from POS/PIM systems. Accepts array of SKU + quantity objects.
